Vision

Graphs, or Networks as we call them, are about studying relationships and their patterns on objects. Since the advent of computing, we have known that a significant portion of human knowledge can be represented and visualised as graphs - without losing the richness of the relationships. Both industry and academia have taken notice of the intuitive advantages of using graphs to systematically represent, model and process large and variable data. If you think about it, the entire world wide web is a giant graph that we can analyse.

The proliferation of graphs as a method of making sense of the world has catalysed the advancement of adjacent graph industries: Graph Analytics, Graph Computing and Graph Databases. At Mindmaps, we are pushing the development of graph based semantic applications by leveraging the inherent advantages of graph databases. Although graph databases have matured and proven to be highly advantageous, they are missing an important element to unlock its full potential: there is no formal way of structuring data contained within a graph. In other words, applications built directly on top of graph databases do not have a robust semantic structure. This issue makes it difficult for your object model to be understandable by the human mind; to grow flexibly as your data model changes; to execute semantic or pattern matching queries; to perform data verification and inference; to utilise data mining functionalities; and to visualise and manage your graph. Mindmaps is designed to solve this problem.
