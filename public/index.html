Mindmaps is a general purpose AI-driven data platform that puts the power of a knowledge graph in the hands of every developer.

At a large scale, current databases could only excel at two out of three key properties of a database: Consistency (semantic correctness of the structure of your data), Flexibility (the ability to adapt, change and scale the structure of your data rapidly) and Expressivity (the ability model problem domains with varying degree of complexity especially in highly interconnected data). Because of this issue, organisations are forced to use multiple databases for different purposes causing data fragmentation and inefficiencies.

Mindmaps platform is inherently consistent, flexible and expressive; thus, it provides a solution to this problem. These characteristics also provide the foundation for us to create an AI driven database to perform reasoning (constraint verification and inference) and analytics. All features of our technology are interfaced directly via Graql, a declarative, general-purpose query language expressly designed for this purpose. Graql is the first and only query language that can not only query, but also reason and perform analytics.

Mindmaps Factory is a semantic abstraction layer that produces a Mindamps graph by structuring your graph database. It gives you the flexibility to decide which database solution best meets your requirements. If you need to scale horizontally, to meet data or traffic volume constraints, or work in memory for speed then Mindmaps Factory will adapt to your needs. Many off-the-shelf graph databases are supported, giving maximum flexibility and allowing you to control your costs. Mindmaps Factory ensures that the database you choose supports all of the features of the platform, leaving you to make your decision purely based on performance.

Graql is the language used to query a Mindmaps graph. It has been designed to complement the object model by working purely in terms of concepts and the relationships between them. This feature allows you to effortlessly create an ontology and to search for the existence of complex patterns in the graph. You do not need to worry about the optimal traversal to find your information; Graql will do this for you.

Graql makes it easy to specify generic constraints on your data that restrict the complex patterns that can exist in the graph. Furthermore, these queries can be extended to define inference rules that expose implicit information in your data to your applications.

Mindmaps Reasoner is a powerful inference engine that can validate constraints on your data and infer new information based on rules specified in Graql. Specifying constraints on your data ensures that it is always in the format you expect in your database. Mission critical analytics can be developed on top of your data without having to worry about its fidelity.

Mindmaps Reasoner uses both forward and backward chaining with data materialisation to make implicit information in your data explicit. Based on patterns specified in Graql, the reasoner identifies matching information in your graph and asserts the logical conclusion.

Once the structure of your graph has been defined, Mindmaps Analytics provides the tools to extract aggregate information. Discover the information contained in the structure of your data using graph analytics. Extract specific data sets amongst highly connected or diverse data. Display common windows onto data by computing statistics. Track and analyse system usage to inform your development and quantify user interaction. Mine your data to identify clusters of products or users and train models to capture and predict behavior.

Interact with all aspects of Mindmaps using our visualisation tool: Mindmaps Insight. You can view and edit your ontology as a graph, making clear how concepts are related and how they are constrained. Navigate your data visually using advanced filtering to rapidly shift focus from the coarsest to the finest levels of detail. Specify graph patterns using an intuitive graphical interface, and execute them as Graql queries to find complex interrelated information. Additionally, you can manage your entire Mindmaps infrastructure using our web UI. Easily configure and scale your infrastructure to meet changing demands in realtime.

Importing your existing data into Mindmaps is simple. If your data is already in RDF triples, it can be mapped into a Mindmaps graph automatically. OWL ontologies can also be transformed into Mindmaps ontologies to preserve existing domain knowledge.

Mindmaps Migration can also be built into existing or custom ETL tools by using Graql to efficiently bulk load your data into a semantic graph. The object model provides the flexibility to import information from disparate sources and across multiple application domains into a single graph.

Let Mindmaps deal with the complex problem of structuring and interacting with your graph database. You can focus on developing your semantic applications while Mindmaps handles the implementation details. Mindmaps Core exposes an object model that semantically structures your data according to an ontology. It abstracts away nodes and edges in your graph and allows your data to be structured in terms of concepts, the relationships between them and the roles they play in these relationships. This simple object model is intuitive to understand and it provides the flexibility to edit your ontology on-the-fly.

Advantages

Mindmaps empowers you to build the type of semantic applications in the way that you want to - from semantic search engines to systems capable of performing complex analytics.

  • Faster Development

    By providing the semantic layer over data stored in a graph, you do not have to build everything from scratch. Our intuitive high level query language, Graql, allows you to easily triangulate the relationships and data points that you want.

  • Flexibility & Modularity

    The different components of Mindmaps can be combined in different ways to suit your intended use case and scalable architecture. Furthermore, you are in control with what database you want to use in order to address your own unique needs and to minimize vendor lock-in.

  • Domain Focus

    Mindmaps allows you to concentrate on your domain ontology and design your architecture around it, since you are no longer encumbered with the low-level implementation of your data. You can now focus on your business application and address your end user needs.

  • Precision Analytics

    The Mindmaps object model is highly expressive, which allows you to model your data more accurately compared to current semantic standards. As a result of the integrity constraints on data and their relationships, mission critical analytics can be built on top of Mindmaps.
