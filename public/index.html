Graphs, or Networks as we call them, are about studying relationships and relationship patterns on objects. For as long as the history of computing exists, we have known that a lot of human knowledge can be represented and viewed as graphs. Graphs and the need to model and analyse them have become so dominant in both academia and industry, that it is found every where today. Google, Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter and many more companies are thriving in the market with data that are represented, modelled and processed as graphs. If you think about it, the entire world wide web is giant graph that we can analyse.

Consequently, this leads to the blooming of graph industries: Graph Analytics, Graph Computing and Graph Databases. At Mindmaps, we are interested in advancing the the development of graph based systems, using graph databases. Although graph databases have matured and proven to be very strong, it is missing an important element: it has no formal way of structuring. In other words, graph databases do not have a strong semantic structure. This problem makes it hard for your object model to be human understable and grow flexibly, perform semantic / pattern matching queries, data verification and inference, develop data mining functionalities and visualise and manage your graph. Mindmaps Stack is developed to solve this problem.

Graql (Graph Query Language) is a declarative language used to query a Mindmaps Graph. There are three main operations that Graql can perform: 'select’ allows patterns to be specified which are searched for in the graph and returned; 'insert' and 'delete' are used to mutate the graph as well as to define inference rules.

Graql has an efficient syntax and is intuitive to use. It is built to complement the Mindmaps Graph object model working purely in terms of concepts and the relationships between them.

Mindmaps reasoner is a powerful inference engine that can be used to validate constraints and infer information based on rules specified in Graql. The reasoner uses both forward and backward chaining with data materialisation to make implicit information in your data explicit.

Importing your existing data into Mindmaps is simple. If your data is in RDF triples already this can be mapped into a mindmaps graph automatically. Any other data can be imported utilising our extract transform layer (ETL) to import from disparate sources. OWL ontologies can also be transformed into Mindmaps ontologies to preserve existing structure.

Mindmaps Core exposes an object model that semantically structures your data. It abstracts away nodes and edges in your graph and allows your data to be structured in terms of concepts, the relationships between them and the roles they play in these relationships.

A domain ontology can be defined and constraints specified to ensure that only valid data will be contained in the database. Inference rules can be added to materialise implicit information in your data for use in queries.

Advantages

  • Faster Development

    By providing the semantic layer over data stored in a graph, you do not have to build everything from scratch. Our intuitive high level query language, Graql, allows you to easily triangulate the relationships and data points that you want.

  • Flexibility & Modularity

    The different components of Mindmaps Stack can be combined in different ways to suit your intended use case and scalable architecture. Furthermore, you are in control with what database you want to use in order to address your own unique needs and to minimize vendor lock in.

  • Domain Focus

    Mindmaps Stack allows you to concentrate on your domain ontology and design your architecture around it since you are no longer encumbered with the low level implementation of your data. You can now focus on your business application and address your end user needs.

  • Precision Analytics

    The Mindmaps object model is highly expressive which allows you to model your data more accurately compared to current semantic standards. As a result of the integrity constraints on data and their relationships, mission critical analytics can be built on top of Mindmaps Stack.
